The **Reports** screen presents the three core financial statements as tabs.
Like Analytics, everything is read-only and derived from your journal — there's
nothing extra to maintain, and the numbers can never drift from your transactions.

![Reports — Income Statement](../../img/reports.png)

## Period selector

Pick the reporting window at the top: **This month**, **Last 3 / 6 / 12 months**,
or **Year to date**. The Income Statement and Cash Flow cover that window; the
Balance Sheet is taken **as of** the window's end date.

## Income Statement

Also called Profit & Loss. Income and expense categories for the period, each with
its total, ending in **Net Income** (income − expenses). Green when you came out
ahead, red when expenses won.

## Balance Sheet

A snapshot **as of** the period end:

- **Assets** — what you own (bank, cash, investment accounts).
- **Liabilities** — what you owe (credit cards, loans).
- **Equity** — opening balances plus **Retained Earnings** (all income minus all
  expenses to date).

By construction it always satisfies the accounting identity
**Assets = Liabilities + Equity**.

## Cash Flow

How much cash moved during the period, per asset account — **Opening**, **Closing**,
and the **Change** — totalled at the bottom, with the period's income and expenses
shown for context.

{{< callout type="info" >}}
Financy doesn't tag transactions as operating, investing, or financing, so this is
a straight cash-movement view rather than a GAAP-style three-section statement.
{{< /callout >}}

{{< callout type="info" >}}
Because the statements are derived, they're always consistent with the journal — fix
a transaction and every report updates instantly.
{{< /callout >}}
